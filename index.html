Really?

Yes, at Berkeley Town House you can:

Belong to a historic housing cooperative

  • Founded in 1960.
  • U.S.’s first-ever senior housing cooperative!
  • Self-governing “cooperative corporation” with 60 shareholders.
  • Each shareholder (“member”) occupies one apartment (“unit”).
  • For tax purposes, you are treated as the owner of your unit.
  • Some banks (e.g., National Cooperative Bank and First Republic) offer mortgages on co-op purchases.

Help run the co-op itself.

How it works

  • Co-op members elect directors.
  • Directors appoint officers, committees, and contractors.
  • Directors budget and charge members monthly assessments to cover costs.
  • Members vote on any changes in fundamental rules; directors vote on subsidiary operating rules.

You can volunteer to be:

  • One of the five directors of the corporation
  • A corporate officer
  • A member of a board-appointed committee
  • A member of an informal committee or working group
  • A one-person performer of good deeds
