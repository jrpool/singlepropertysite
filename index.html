At BTH, you can …

Belong to a historic
housing cooperative.

  • BTH was founded in 1960.
  • It is the U.S.’s first-ever senior housing cooperative!
  • It is a self-governing “cooperative corporation” with 60 shareholders.
  • Each shareholder (“member”) occupies one apartment (“unit”).
  • For tax purposes, you are treated as the owner of your unit.

At BTH, you can …

Help run the co-op.

How it works:

  • Co-op members elect directors.
  • Directors appoint officers, committees, and contractors and approve new members. pic
  • Directors budget and charge members monthly assessments to cover costs.
  • Members vote on any changes in fundamental rules; directors vote on subsidiary operating rules. pic

Buying in to BTH:

How it works:

  • You are eligible if at least one 55+ person in your household intends to make BTH a permanent primary residence (Calif. Civil Code Sec. 51.3).
  • If you have a trust, it may buy on your behalf.
  • You negotiate a price and terms with a current shareholder.
  • You get seller disclosures about the apartment and BTH.
  • You can apply to National Cooperative Bank or First Republic for a co-op home loan (“share loan”).
  • You get screened, interviewed, and approved by the BTH directors.

Yes, please contact me about this.
